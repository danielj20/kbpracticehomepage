6 of the Best Websites to Practice For and Prepare For the Knowledge Bowl Competition

Finding Resources For Knowledge Bowl

Knowledge Bowl can be a difficult thing to study for. Not only is it only able to truly be practiced with a group, but there has historically not been very many ways to practice Knowledge Bowl Online - which would be very valuable indeed. Imagine if instead of having to get together with friends every time you wanted to brush up on your knowledge or get better at buzzing all you could do was type a domain name into the search bar and instantly be practicing. Well, now you can. But this website isn’t the only way to gain knowledge about trivia. Here are six of the most important websites.

The List

  • kbpractice.com - Made by a high school student looking for new resources for Knowledge Bowl, this site is the closest thing you’ll get to actually being able to play Knowledge Bowl online. It pulls from a database of over 10,000 questions, is available 24/7, and simulates buzzing and answering questions in real time.
  • sporcle.com - This website allows you to learn trivia relating to any topic - from Medieval Europe to obscure knowledge about Dumbledore’s army. No matter what you specialize in on your Knowledge Bowl team, there will be resources you can find on this website.
  • NAQT's You Gotta Know - This website was created by the organization that runs quizbowl and provides hundreds of topics to learn about and review your knowledge of with a written summary to get you feeling more comfortable for your next Knowledge Bowl meet.
  • brainscape.com - Ever wanted to simply build your general knowledge? Do you want to quiz yourself on a certain topic, but don’t know how to make the flashcards for it? Brainscape can help with that. It has hundreds of premade official flashcard sets for anything you might want to learn about.
  • quizlet.com - Like Brainscape, Quizlet is another flashcards app. Quizlet is great for customization and ensuring that the deck you’re creating will properly prepare you for whatever you’re specializing in in the next Knowledge Bowl meet.
  • protobowl.com - Made to help people across the world practice quizbowl, this site will increase your worldview and allow you to familiarize yourself with many of the terms of a given topic.

Practice, Practice, Practice

Now that these websites have been provided, you need to incorporate them into your daily routine and practice in order to see the full effect of them in a Knowledge Bowl meet. Also, a general understanding of the topics you’re learning about always pays off more than rot memorization. It’s unlikely Knowledge Bowl will ask the exact same questions you’ve studied, so make sure you can actually comprehend what you’re learning.
